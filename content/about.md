## Why I'm Doing This

### The Corporate IT Problem

I'm grateful for my jobâ€”it pays well, it's stable, and I work with smart people. But there's a fundamental problem with corporate IT:

**Velocity dies in bureaucracy.**

- Every idea needs 3 layers of approval
- Innovation happens in PowerPoint, not in production
- Technology choices are made by committees, not builders
- Projects are measured in quarters, not sprints
- Risk aversion kills experimentation

I'm not complainingâ€”I understand why large organizations work this way. But I'm also not willing to spend the next 20 years asking permission to build things.

### The AI-Powered Opportunity

AI pair programming changed the equation for solo builders:

**Before AI:**
- Need co-founder or team (coordination overhead)
- Need to choose: scope OR speed (painful tradeoffs)
- Technical debt accumulates fast (solo developers burn out)

**With AI (Claude Code, Claude, etc.):**
- Build full-stack products solo (no coordination needed)
- Velocity is limited by ideas, not hands (AI writes code fast)
- Quality stays high (AI helps with patterns, testing, docs)

**The new equation: Time + AI + Focus = Products**
